Visual language & design decisions

Edges & corners

This brand uses sharp, intentional geometry. Stick to a maximum of 4px radius on interactive elements like buttons and inputs. Avoid anything pill-shaped or overly rounded — it conflicts with the brand's structured, confident tone.

Use: 0–4px radius
Do
Get started

Buttons at 4px. Cards at 4px. Input fields at 0px or 2px.

Don't
Get started

Avoid pill buttons and heavily rounded cards. Feels off-brand.

Borders vs. shadows

Use thin 1px borders to separate surfaces. Reserve drop shadows for overlays and modals only — never use them to elevate cards or sections. Flat, bordered layouts feel more refined for this brand.

Use: borders for separation
Do

1px border at ~18% opacity. Clean and flat.

Don't

Heavy drop shadows on cards feel too generic and soft.

Spacing & breathing room

Give elements room to breathe. Generous whitespace is a core part of the brand's sophisticated feel. Err on the side of more space between sections and components — never crowd elements together.

Use: generous margins & padding
Do
Action

Spacious padding. 24px+ gaps between elements.

Don't
Action

Cramped spacing undermines the premium feel.

Dividers & separators

When you need to separate content, use a hairline — 1px at low opacity. Prefer whitespace alone wherever possible. Never use thick rules, decorative ornaments, or full-bleed colored dividers to break up sections.

Use: 1px lines or whitespace only
Do

1px hairline at ~12% opacity. Subtle and refined.

Don't

Thick or colored rules feel heavy and dated.

Color on surfaces

Use brand colors as background blocks, not as text highlights or decorative fills on small elements. The primary green should anchor large surface areas — not be sprinkled throughout as accents. Keep most surfaces in cream or white.

Use: color in large blocks
Do

Full-bleed green section, cream below. Confident blocks.

Don't
Tag

Sprinkling green on icons, tags, and bullets dilutes the brand.
